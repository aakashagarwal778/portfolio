Snapshot

A complete first-principles derivation of Geometric Brownian Motion — from the multiplicative structure of asset prices through the log-normal distribution, Itô's Lemma, and both the expectation and PDE routes to the Black-Scholes formula. Every step shown explicitly with no hand-waving. Paired with an executable Jupyter notebook demonstrating the two-routes principle numerically. Concludes with a survey of GBM extensions: OU, CIR, Heston, Merton jump-diffusion, and regime-switching.

Central result
\(S_T = S_0\exp\!\bigl[(\mu - \tfrac{1}{2}\sigma^2)T + \sigma W_T\bigr]\)
The −½σ² correction is where GBM and naïve calculus diverge — Jensen's inequality made explicit.
Black-Scholes formula (both routes)
\(C = SN(d_1) - Ke^{-rT}N(d_2)\)
Derived via expectation route (risk-neutral measure) and PDE route (heat equation). Same answer.
