USD/TRY Under Sanctions Shock: Time-Varying Jump Intensity and Geopolitical Tension Signal

Geoeconomic SDE Series · Case 02 LaTeX PDF · Calibration data Data: USD/TRY · 5,459 obs · 2000–2026 June 2025
Download PDF ↗ View Python code ↗
Snapshot

USD/TRY does not drift smoothly — it depreciates persistently, then breaks sharply around identifiable geopolitical events. The clustering of large moves around sanctions announcements, central bank governor firings, and OFAC designations cannot be captured by a constant jump intensity. This document derives a jump-diffusion SDE with time-varying \(\lambda(t)\) driven by a geopolitical tension signal, calibrates it on 5,459 daily observations, and produces regime-conditional probability distributions at the 1-year horizon.

Key result — 1-year horizon from USD/TRY = 38
95th pct: 69.4 (calm) vs 71.6 (tension)
Regime signal is in the tail, not the center — tension shifts extreme outcomes without moving the median materially
Core SDE
\(dX = \mu\,dt + \sigma\,dW + J\,dq(\lambda(t))\)
\(\lambda(t) = \lambda_0 + (\lambda_1-\lambda_0)\cdot s(t)\) — time-varying intensity breaks Merton tractability → Euler-Maruyama required
