Ornstein-Uhlenbeck Process and Mean-Reversion: A Theoretical Foundation

Math Finance Series · Document 02 LaTeX PDF · Jupyter notebook Repo: stochastic-finance 2024

Snapshot

A complete derivation of the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process — from the motivation for stationarity through the integrating factor solution, Fokker-Planck stationary distribution, MLE calibration, and bond pricing under the Vasicek model. Establishes the OU process as the unique continuous-time process that is simultaneously Markov, Gaussian, and stationary, and covers the key limitation (negative values) that motivates the CIR extension.

Closed-form solution
\(X_t = \theta + (X_0-\theta)e^{-\kappa t} + \sigma\!\int_0^t\! e^{-\kappa(t-s)}dW_s\)
Exact — no Euler-Maruyama required when diffusion is constant.
Stationary distribution (Fokker-Planck)
\(X_\infty \sim \mathcal{N}(\theta,\,\sigma^2/2\kappa)\)
Half-life: \(\tau_{1/2} = \log(2)/\kappa\) — directly calibratable, directly interpretable.
